Virginia Code — Key Sections
Citation What It Does Key Details Effective
§ 18.2-287.4:1 Transfer prohibition (HB217/SB749) Creates the main transfer ban — prohibits sale, purchase, or transfer of covered firearms after July 1, 2026. Includes grandfathering clause and carve-outs for licensed dealers, out-of-state buyers, immediate family gifts, gunsmiths, and inheritance. Jul 1, 2026
§ 18.2-287.4 Public-carry ban (HB1524/SB727) Prohibits carrying a covered firearm "on or about his person" on public streets, sidewalks, roads, parks, and all places open to the public. Exempts law enforcement (on duty), military (on duty), and persons actively engaged in lawful hunting or range activities. Jul 1, 2026
§ 18.2-308.2:2 Feature definition Defines which features trigger coverage for semi-automatic centerfire rifles, pistols, and shotguns. The "two features" test for rifles: folding/telescoping stock, pistol grip, thumbhole stock, second handgrip, grenade/flare launcher, flash suppressor, muzzle brake, threaded barrel, bayonet mount, bipod. Jul 1, 2026
§ 18.2-308.7 Age restrictions (HB1525/CHAP1102) Persons under 21 may not purchase a covered firearm. Persons under 18 may not possess one (narrow military/law enforcement/cadet exceptions). Ages 18-20 who lawfully owned before July 1, 2026 may continue to possess. Jul 1, 2026
§ 18.2-308(C) Transport exemptions A set of transport exemptions that apply mutatis mutandis to covered firearms. Key ones: (C)(3) going to/from a range, unloaded and wrapped; (C)(8) secured in container or compartment in a personal vehicle — no specific destination required; (C)(9) going to/from firearms training. Pre-existing
§ 18.2-308.2:5 Private sale background check Requires background checks for all private firearm sales (not just covered firearms). A Lynchburg court dissolved a prior injunction blocking this on June 25, 2026. Also separately prohibits selling a covered firearm for any consideration. Jul 1, 2026
§ 18.2-309.1 Penalties for unlawful transfer Jul 1, 2026 Class 1 misdemeanor (max 12 months jail, $2,500 fine) for unlawful transfer or possession of a covered firearm. Conviction triggers a 3-year federal firearms purchase disability under 18 U.S.C. § 922(n).
Title 64.2 Wills, trusts & estates Virginia's inheritance and estate law. Governs bequests of covered firearms. The inheritance carve-out in § 18.2-287.4:1(C)(vii) has no family restriction — any non-prohibited heir named in a will may inherit. Pre-existing

Crump v. Katz — Lancaster County Circuit Court
Virginia state court
Injunction Granted — Jun 25, 2026
Blocks Virginia State Police from enforcing the transfer ban. Effect is limited to VSP — local Commonwealth's Attorneys and police departments not named in the case are not bound. Injunction expires December 31, 2026.
Santolla v. Katz — Washington County Circuit Court
Virginia state court
Injunction Granted — Jun 29, 2026
Blocks VSP plus six named Commonwealth's Attorneys from enforcing. Despite some news coverage calling this "statewide," it is not — it binds only the named parties. Injunction runs through July 1, 2027. The Virginia Supreme Court declined to consolidate the four state cases on July 6, 2026.

Virginia state court
Pending — No Ruling Yet
Injunction motion pending. No ruling as of July 7, 2026. Assume the law is enforceable in Fauquier County until the court rules.
Curtis v. Katz — Spotsylvania County Circuit Court
Virginia state court

The court declined to block enforcement. The law is in full effect in Spotsylvania County.

Federal Law
Citation What It Does Relevance to This Tool
18 U.S.C. § 922(a)(5) Restrictions on private transfers to out-of-state residents A private citizen generally cannot directly transfer a firearm to a resident of another state. Transfer must go through a licensed dealer (FFL) in the buyer's state.
18 U.S.C. § 922(b)(3) Dealer transfer requirements Licensed dealers may only transfer long guns to residents of the state where the dealer is located, or to out-of-state residents in face-to-face transactions where the sale is legal in both states. Governs FFL-to-out-of-state-buyer transfers.
18 U.S.C. § 922(n) Firearms purchase disability — misdemeanor conviction A person convicted of a crime punishable by imprisonment for more than one year (including Virginia Class 1 misdemeanors, which carry up to 12 months) may be subject to a 3-year federal firearms purchase disability. Relevant to the penalty for violating HB217.
18 U.S.C. § 926A Interstate transport safe passage A person may transport a firearm interstate if (1) the possession is legal at origin and destination, (2) the firearm is unloaded, and (3) the firearm is in a locked container. Relevant to Virginia residents traveling out of state with a grandfathered firearm.
