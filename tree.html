Scenario Key Condition Result
Age 21+ · Owned before July 1, 2026
 Grandfathered Permitted
Protected by the grandfathering clause in § 18.2-287.4:1. Can keep and use for personal purposes.
Age 18–20 · Owned before July 1, 2026
 Grandfathered Permitted
Grandfathered. HB1525 restricts purchase (age 21+) but not possession for 18–20.
Under 18 · Owned before July 1, 2026
 Under 18 Unsettled
Grandfathering may conflict with the existing under-18 assault firearm possession ban in § 18.2-308.7. Not yet resolved by courts.
Age 21+ · Acquired on/after July 1, 2026
 Post-ban Prohibited
Acquisition and continued possession are unlawful. Class 1 misdemeanor; 3-year firearms disability on conviction.
Age 18–20 · Acquired on/after July 1, 2026
 Post-ban · Under 21 Prohibited
No lawful path to acquire — both HB217 and HB1525 prohibit this.
Under 18 · Acquired on/after July 1, 2026
 Post-ban · Under 18 Prohibited
Prohibited under both HB217 and § 18.2-308.7.
Scenario Key Condition Result
Gift to immediate family · Grandfathered firearm · Recipient age 21+
Spouse, child, parent, grandparent, or sibling — recipient not prohibited from owning firearms
 Family · Pre-July 1 Permitted
Explicit carve-out § 18.2-287.4:1(C)(x). No background check required for a gift.
Gift to immediate family member age 18–20 · Grandfathered
Recipient is 18–20, a close relative, not otherwise prohibited
 Family · 18–20 Permitted
Recipient can possess (not prohibited). HB1525 only restricts purchase for 18–20 — not receipt by gift.
Gift to immediate family member · Under 18 recipient
 Under 18 Prohibited
Under-18 recipient is prohibited from possessing under § 18.2-308.7 — carve-out condition is not met.
Gift to any person · Post-July 1 firearm
 Not grandfathered Prohibited
Only grandfathered firearms can be gifted. A post-ban firearm was acquired unlawfully to begin with.
Gift to a friend or non-family Virginia resident
Not a spouse, child, parent, grandparent, or sibling
 Non-family Prohibited
Family gift exception is limited to immediate family. Non-family transfer = Class 1 misdemeanor.
Transfer to a licensed gun dealer (FFL)
 FFL Permitted
Explicit FFL carve-out. Dealer takes responsibility for any onward transfer.
Surrender to law enforcement or a buyback program
 Law enforcement Permitted
Voluntary surrender is an explicit carve-out in HB217. No penalty.
Scenario Key Condition Result
Sell or consign to a licensed gun dealer (FFL)
 FFL Permitted
Explicit carve-out. Grandfathered firearms only. Dealer handles any onward sale.
Sell to an out-of-state buyer — via FFL in buyer's state
Buyer takes possession in their home state through a federally licensed dealer
 Out-of-state · FFL Conditional
Must go through buyer's FFL — federal law prohibits direct hand-off across state lines (18 U.S.C. § 922).
Sell to a family member in Virginia (paid)
Even to a spouse, child, parent, or sibling — for money, goods, or anything of value
 Family sale Prohibited
The family carve-out says "gift" — a paid sale to family is separately prohibited under § 18.2-308.2:5(E).
Private sale to any Virginia resident
 Private VA sale Prohibited
No private in-state sale exception. Class 1 misdemeanor; 3-year firearms disability on conviction.
Any sale — post-July 1 firearm
 Not grandfathered Prohibited
Post-ban acquisition is itself illegal — there is nothing lawful to sell.
Surrender to law enforcement / buyback program
Compensation received from a buyback does not make this a prohibited "sale"
 Buyback Permitted
Explicit carve-out. Buyback compensation is treated separately from a commercial sale.
Scenario Key Condition Result
Inherit from a family member
Decedent lawfully owned the firearm before death; heir is not prohibited from owning firearms
 Inheritance Permitted
Explicit carve-out § 18.2-287.4:1(C)(vii). No family restriction on who may inherit — any named heir qualifies.
Non-family heir inherits by will or trust
Named in a will — not a close relative — and not prohibited from owning firearms
 Non-family heir Permitted
The inheritance carve-out has no family limitation. A non-family devisee under a will qualifies.
Bequeath by will to any heir (family or non-family)
Leaving a grandfathered assault firearm to anyone who is not prohibited from owning firearms
 Bequest Permitted
Plan ahead: name the specific firearm in your will. An estate-planning attorney can help structure the bequest correctly.
Scenario Key Condition Result
In a personal vehicle — secured in a locked container or compartment
No specific destination required — covers general vehicle transport on public roads
 Vehicle · Secured Permitted
§ 18.2-308(C)(8) applies mutatis mutandis. Not limited to specific destinations.
On private property — with owner's permission
 Private land Permitted
Public-carry ban applies only to public streets, sidewalks, parks, and places open to the public.
Going to or from a shooting range or hunting area
On foot: weapon unloaded and securely wrapped. In a vehicle: secured in a container or compartment.
 Range / Hunt travel Conditional
Exemption confirmed for travel to/from an established range (§ 18.2-308(C)(3)) or for vehicle transport (C)(8). Walking to a hunting spot on a public road remains a gap.
Actively hunting or at an established shooting range
Actually engaged in the lawful activity — not just traveling to it
 Active hunting / range Permitted
HB1524 explicitly exempts persons "actually engaged in lawful hunting or lawful recreational shooting activities at an established shooting range or shooting contest."
Walking in public — street, sidewalk, or park
Carrying openly or concealed in a public place not connected to hunting or a range visit
 Public carry Unsettled
Generally prohibited by HB1524. Some § 18.2-308 exemptions may apply but their full scope for assault firearms has not been definitively resolved.
Scenario Key Condition Result
Virginia resident taking their own grandfathered gun out of state
Secured in vehicle; check destination state's laws
 Leaving VA Conditional
Legal to leave Virginia. Federal safe passage (18 U.S.C. § 926A) applies for interstate travel where firearm is legal at both origin and destination, unloaded, in a locked container. Always check destination state law.
Virginia resident returning home with their own gun
Owned it before July 1, 2026; took it out of state temporarily; now coming back
 Returning home Permitted
Grandfathering is tied to lawful ownership — not physical location in Virginia. Taking it out of state doesn't change its status.
Virginia resident who bought a gun out of state to bring home
Purchased after July 1, 2026 — intending to transport it into Virginia
 Out-of-state purchase Prohibited
Prohibited by both Virginia's import ban and federal law (18 U.S.C. § 922(a)(3)).
Non-Virginia resident visiting with their own gun
Temporary visitor bringing a lawfully owned assault firearm into Virginia
 Non-resident visitor Unsettled
No confirmed visitor exception in HB217. The import ban may apply to any person bringing a covered firearm into Virginia. Federal safe passage may not cover an extended stay. Consult an attorney before traveling.
Scenario Key Condition Result
Keep a magazine you already owned before July 1, 2026
 Grandfathered mag Permitted
§ 18.2-308.1:9 grandfathers pre-existing high-capacity magazines for continued possession only.
Sell, trade, or transfer a high-capacity magazine to anyone
 Transfer Prohibited
Grandfathering covers possession only — not transfer. Selling, trading, or otherwise giving away a >15 round magazine after July 1, 2026 is prohibited.
Gift a high-capacity magazine to immediate family
Whether the § 18.2-287.4:1(C)(x) family gift exception extends to magazines separately governed by § 18.2-308.1:9
 Family mag gift Unsettled
Bill text is ambiguous on whether the family gift carve-out for assault firearms also applies to magazines. Consult an attorney before proceeding.
Scenario Key Condition Result
Served with a qualifying protective order or convicted of misdemeanor domestic assault (effective July 1, 2026)
HB93/SB38 (CHAP 533/534) — applies to ALL firearms, not just assault firearms
 HB93/SB38 Required Transfer
Must transfer ALL firearms within 24 hours via one of three paths: (1) surrender to law enforcement, (2) sell/transfer to a licensed FFL, or (3) transfer to a non-prohibited person age 21+ not in the same household. File written certification with the court clerk within 48 hours.
