Informational analysis under HB217/SB749, HB1524/SB727, and HB1525 — assumes law fully in effect as of July 1, 2026

Enforcement Status — July 6, 2026: Four Cases Proceeding Separately

Two state courts have issued preliminary injunctions; two have not. The U.S. DOJ filed a federal challenge July 1. The Virginia Supreme Court declined on July 6 to consolidate the four state cases — each proceeds independently in its own circuit. AG Jones states the ban is in effect in the remaining ~127 jurisdictions. This tool answers as if the law is fully in effect.

July 6: Virginia Supreme Court declined to consolidate the four state cases. Appeals proceed separately in each circuit. Consolidation at the Court of Appeals level remains possible.

U.S. Supreme Court — Cert Granted June 30, 2026. SCOTUS consolidated Viramontes v. Cook County (7th Cir.) and Grant v. Higgins (2nd Cir.) to decide whether AR-15-pattern rifles are protected under the Second Amendment. Argument this fall; decision expected spring 2027. A ruling against those bans would almost certainly invalidate Virginia’s law.
Private-Sale Background Checks Now in Effect — July 1, 2026

HB1525 revived universal background check requirements for private firearm transfers. A Lynchburg Circuit Court dissolved the prior injunction June 25. VSP NICS checks for private sales resumed July 1. Applies to all firearms, not only assault firearms.
