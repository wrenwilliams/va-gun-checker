Courts are fighting over this law right now

Some courts have blocked enforcement — but not everywhere. In most of Virginia, the ban is still in effect. This tool answers as if the full law applies. Talk to a lawyer about your specific county.

  • Crump v. Katz — Lancaster County Circuit CourtInjunction GRANTED June 25 — Blocks Virginia State Police from enforcing the ban. Expires Dec 31, 2026.
  • Santolla v. Katz — Washington County Circuit CourtInjunction GRANTED June 29 — Blocks VSP + 6 named prosecutors. Delays the law to July 1, 2027 in those jurisdictions. Despite news reports, this is not a statewide injunction — it only binds the named defendants.
  • Black v. Hook — Fauquier County Circuit CourtPENDING — No ruling yet.
  • Curtis v. Katz — Spotsylvania County Circuit CourtInjunction DENIED June 18 — The ban is enforceable in Spotsylvania.
  • United States v. Virginia — U.S. District Court (E.D. Va.)FILED July 1 — Federal TRO pending — DOJ Second Amendment challenge. Scope TBD.
U.S. Supreme Court — Cert Granted June 30, 2026. SCOTUS took up two cases about whether AR-15-style rifles are protected by the Second Amendment. Arguments this fall; decision expected spring 2027. If the Court rules against similar bans, Virginia's law would likely fall too. July 6: Virginia Supreme Court declined to consolidate the four state cases — each one proceeds separately.

Private sale background checks are back — as of July 1, 2026

If you sell any firearm privately in Virginia (not just assault firearms), the buyer must now pass a background check. A court had blocked this rule, but a Lynchburg judge dissolved that injunction June 25.
